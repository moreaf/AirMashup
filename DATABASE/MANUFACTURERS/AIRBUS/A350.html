Development and production

The Airbus A350 XWB is a family of long-range, twin-engine wide-body jet airliners developed by the
European aerospace manufacturer Airbus. The A350 is the first Airbus aircraft with both fuselage and
wing structures made primarily of carbon fibre reinforced polymer. Its variants seat 315 to 369
passengers in typical seating layouts. The A350 is positioned to succeed the A340 and to compete with
the Boeing 787 and 777.

The A350 was originally conceived in 2004 as a pairing of the A330's fuselage with new aerodynamics
features and engines. In 2006, Airbus redesigned the aircraft in response to negative feedback from
several major prospective customers, producing the "A350 XWB" (eXtra Wide Body). Development costs
are estimated at €11 billion (US$15 billion or £9.5 billion). As of February 2019, Airbus had received 893
orders for A350s from 51 customers worldwide.[5] The prototype A350 first flew on 14 June 2013 from
Toulouse, France. Type certification from the European Aviation Safety Agency was received in
September 2014 and certification from the Federal Aviation Administration two months later. On 15
January 2015, the A350-900 entered service with its launch operator Qatar Airways, and the A350-1000
on 24 February 2018 with the same airline.
